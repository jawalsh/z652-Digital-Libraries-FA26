# Z652 Final Project — Rubric

_Assesses ability to design, implement, and evaluate a prototype digital library using CollectionBuilder, applying advanced metadata standards and reflecting on technical and curatorial choices._

| Criteria | 4 – Exceptional | 3 – Good | 2 – Acceptable | 1 – Unacceptable |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Completeness and Relevance** | Project fully meets all stated requirements, including minimum collection size, metadata fields, contextual content, evaluation memo, and video presentation. | Meets most requirements with minor omissions. | Meets some requirements but with significant gaps in deliverables. | Fails to meet basic requirements. |
| **Implementation & Technical Quality** | CollectionBuilder site fully functional; high-quality digital assets; effective use of advanced features (e.g., “Feature Includes”); interface is polished, consistent, and user-friendly. | Functional site with good-quality assets and mostly consistent design; limited use of advanced features. | Basic functionality with technical or usability issues; inconsistent asset quality. | Incomplete, non-functional, or poor-quality implementation. |
| **Metadata Quality & Standards** | Metadata exceeds baseline Dublin Core; uses controlled vocabularies and content standards appropriately; rights/use statements and technical credits follow best practices. | Metadata meets baseline requirements with some additional descriptive fields and controlled vocabulary usage. | Metadata minimal, inconsistent, or missing controlled vocabulary application. | Metadata incomplete, inaccurate, or missing entirely. |
| **Copyright and Metadata Awareness** | Copyright status addressed with reference to specific holders, dates, or public domain guidelines; metadata sources and challenges realistically assessed; implications for discovery, reuse, and access are considered. | Copyright and metadata issues addressed with some detail; minor gaps in accuracy or scope. | Mentions copyright and/or metadata issues but with limited detail or understanding. | No meaningful discussion of copyright or metadata issues. |
| **Contextualization & Narrative Depth** | Introductory and exhibit pages provide strong research-based framing, clear orientation, and analysis of collection’s significance and user needs. | Contextual materials provide adequate framing and orientation. | Contextualization minimal or purely descriptive. | No contextual or interpretive content. |
| **Evaluation Memo** | Professionally formatted; clear memo style; provides detailed, insightful evaluation of platform, process, and outcomes; offers actionable recommendations. | Memo covers required points with some insight and recommendations. | Memo mostly descriptive; lacks depth or actionable recommendations. | Missing, incomplete, or unprofessional memo. |
| **Professionalism & Mechanics** | All components polished, error-free, and submitted on time; citations and attributions accurate. | Mostly polished; minor errors; citations generally correct. | Noticeable errors; incomplete or inconsistent citations. | Poorly written; citations absent or incorrect. |
