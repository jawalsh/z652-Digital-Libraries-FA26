# Week 1: Introduction to Digital Libraries

## Summary

This week introduces the course and begins our work with digital libraries and digital collections. We will use this first week to get oriented, review the course structure and major assignments, and begin looking closely at how digital collections are organized, described, and presented to users.

Because this is an asynchronous week, the emphasis is on orientation and observation rather than technical setup. You will watch a brief course welcome video, review the syllabus and final project overview, browse examples of digital collections, and complete a short response activity. You do not need to have a final project idea yet, but you should begin thinking about kinds of materials, communities, topics, or collections that might interest you.

Later in the course, we will build small digital collections using CollectionBuilder. This week begins that process by asking some basic questions: What is a digital library? What kinds of objects do digital libraries contain? What role does metadata play? What do digital collection interfaces make easy or difficult for users?

## Weekly Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you should be able to:

- *describe* the general goals, structure, and major assignments of the course.
- *define* **digital object** and *describe* a specific example of a digital object.
- *explain* the importance of **metadata** in digital collections.
- *identify* visible **metadata** elements in different digital library interfaces.
- *analyze* basic features of a digital collection interface, including objects, users, metadata, search, browse, and access.
- *begin identifying* possible materials, topics, or collection types for your final project.

2. Who do you think the intended users are?
